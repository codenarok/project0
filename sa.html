Serie A teams start group training

Now that the medical protocol has been approved, most Serie A teams are already training in groups, but some are still keeping social distancing until Monday. The FIGC, Government, Lega Serie A and Playersâ€™ Association finally reached an agreement on the medical protocol for contact training this week, after several versions were rejected. It has given them the option of working in games between ranks and reducing social distancing, but not everyone has taken that opportunity up yet.

Udinese started group training today, joining Inter, Milan, Napoli, Roma, Fiorentina, Sassuolo, Parma, Cagliari, Atalanta, Torino, Genoa and Lecce. The teams still maintaining some social distancing guidelines in training until next week are Juventus, Lazio, Hellas Verona, Brescia, Sampdoria, SPAL and Bologna.
